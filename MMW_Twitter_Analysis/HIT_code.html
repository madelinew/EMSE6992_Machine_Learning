Determine if the tweet indicates that the person who posted it/retweeted it approves texting/using a cell phone while driving or disapproves texting/using a cell phone while driving.

Examples of Approves:

  • The tweet says “I am driving right now”.
  • The tweet does not support a texting while driving law.
  • The tweet supports others texting and driving.

Examples of Disapproves:

  • The tweet says “Don’t text and drive”.
  • The tweet says “Say no to distracted driving”.
  • The tweet mentions seeing somebody else texting and driving and gives a negative view point about them doing it.
  • Tweets involving statistics based on texting and driving that are based on deaths.
  • Tweets that are concerning new laws or bills being passed based on texting and driving without the person stating they disapprove them.

Examples of Neither/Unknown:

  • The tweet/retweet says “My girlfriend is driving me crazy”.
  • The tweet/retweet says “I love texting my mom”.
  • The tweet/retweet is cut off before a mention of using a cell phone while driving.
  • The tweet/retweet mentions seeing somebody use their cell phone while driving but does not state their opinion on the matter (either through text or emojis).
